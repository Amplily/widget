Key Moments

  • Major Forces Driving Investments in SDN

    There has been an increased demand for data center agility, to meet the speed of the software itself. But traditional networks are getting in the way. For a new application, compute and storage may be spun up in minutes, only to be delayed by the network for days or weeks.

  • How Cisco ACI Fundamentally Changes the Network Landscape

    As a leader in software-defined networking, Cisco ACI is fundamentally changing the landscape of the data center by separating the control plane from the forwarding plane, resulting in centralized management. This reduces provisioning times and provides more granular control of the environment.

  • An Analogy for Understanding Cisco ACI

    Himanshu from Cisco describes how Cisco ACI borrows a concept from the cell carrier network. SIM cards enable smartphones to act as a stateless networking devices that receive their identities and network configuration from a SIM card.

  • Impact and Challenges for Network Operations Teams

    Himanshu discusses the operational challenges network teams may face as the adopt a new technology as disruptive as Cisco ACI.

  • How the NetBrain and Cisco ACI Joint Solution Works

    NetBrain discovers and models the Cisco ACI fabric through API integration to Cisco's APIC controller. NetBrain augments that data with information discovered through the traditional network, via SSH/telnet and SNMP. This allows teams to map their networks end-to-end, and manage the hybrid infrastructure in a familiar and consistent manner.

  • Using a Network Map as the Single Pane of Glass

    Jason discusses how a Dynamic Map can ask as the user interface for network management. Virtually any data accessible from the APIC controller, SSH/Telnet, and any other network management solution can be accessed directly from the map, for any task.

  • Demo: Mapping Cisco ACI alongside the Traditional Network

    David runs a live demo, illustrating how to map the Cisco ACI fabric alongside the traditional infrastructure it connects to in the data center. David also shows how data from other tools can be accessed directly from the map.

  • Transitioning to an Application Centric Mindset

    Operations teams will need to embrace change to successfully take over an ACI environment. Just as the network must evolve to the needs of the app, teams will need to adapt to an app-centric mindset. With ACI,the boundary between the app and the network is more blurred than ever.

  • Demo: Application Centric View of ACI

    David runs a live demo to show how operations teams can map their network in the context of a specific application, to better visualize the mapping between the overlay and underlay network.

  • Leveraging Automation to Troubleshoot Cisco ACI

    For any given application, engineers must be able to understand what are the endpoints, which ports do they connect to, and what devices are along the path. The abstract and dynamic nature of Cisco ACI environments make these questions very difficult to answer using traditional troubleshooting methods.

  • Demo: Troubleshooting an abstract and dynamic ACI environment

    In this demo, David shows how to you might troubleshoot an app running across the ACI fabric by mapping the application flows end-to-end, across the infrastructure. He also shows how a user can search for an application to instantly map its dependencies to the underlying network.
